Wouter M. Kouw

Post-doctoral Researcher

TU Eindhoven


I am a post-doctoral researcher at the Bayesian Intelligent Autonomous Systems lab in the Signal Processing Systems group of the TU Eindhoven.

My main interests are machine intelligence and computational neuroscience. My work has looked at intelligent systems that aim to learn from a source domain and generalize to a target domain, known as domain-adaptive classifiers. In my core projects, I focus on designing and analyzing estimators. Insights I gain are applied to problems in image, signal and natural language processing. Currently, I am working on active inference agents, using variational inference on factor graphs.

The problem of domain adaptation in machine learning

Intelligent systems are deployed for a wide array of tasks, such as diagnosing patients, recognizing faces or translating texts [1]. They are called “intelligent” because these machines are not specifically programmed to perform the task. Instead, they learn to do their job from a “training set” of examples. For example, the system might be told to diagnose heart disease in patients based on biometrics such as age and cholesterol level.
