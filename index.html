Biography

I am interested in robots, brains and robotbrains. Intelligent behaviour fascinates me: how animals can efficiently process a flood of sensory signals and learn to take actions to survive. I’m passionate about taking what we know from how brains process information, to making intelligent machines.

I work in the Bayesian Intelligent Autonomous Systems lab of the TU Eindhoven. We design active inference agents; intelligent systems that sense their environment, make predictions and take actions, based on the principle of free energy. My goal is to make inference more efficient through concepts from neural computation. At the moment, I want to make individual nodes in an agent’s factor graph decide for themselves to pass their message forwards.
