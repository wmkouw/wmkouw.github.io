Wouter M. Kouw

Post-doctoral Researcher

TU Eindhoven

I am a researcher interested in robots, brains and robotbrains. I’m fascinated by intelligent behaviour: how animals can efficiently process tons of sensory signals and learn to take actions to survive. I’m passionate about taking what we know from how brains process information, to making intelligent machines.

I work in the Bayesian Intelligent Autonomous Systems lab of the TU Eindhoven. The lab focuses on [active inference agents, which are systems that use the principle of free energy minimization to process information, make predictions and take actions. My work aims to to make inference more efficient, through making individual nodes in the agent’s factor graph decide for themselves if they will communicate a signal forward, based on how much free energy reduction the message produces.

Previous projects include studying the limits of generalizing to data from other distributions (domain-adaptive machine learning) and cross-validation under covariate shift. Mainly, I designed and analyzed maximum-likelihood, minimax and minimum-variance estimators for statistical models, which are applied to problems in image, signal and natural language processing.
